Evidence grading

grade A — Two or more independent replications, zero contradictions. The strongest observational evidence.

grade B — A single supporting study, no contradictions. Preliminary signal worth following up.

grade D — Contradictions present across studies. Inconsistent or conflicting evidence.

⚗ causal (mouse) — Germ-free mouse colonization study. Causal evidence in an animal model, not just correlation.

⚡ RCT — Randomized controlled trial. The strongest available human evidence for causality.

Data sources

Disbiome — Primary disease-association source. Curates case-control studies from the published literature.

BugSigDB — Second independent disease-association source. Differential-abundance signatures from peer-reviewed studies. Quality-filtered to case-control, cross-sectional, prospective cohort, and RCT designs with real sample sizes and signatures ≤40 taxa.

Maier et al. 2018 — Drug interactions. 1,197 compounds screened against 38 gut bacteria (Nature, FDR p<0.01).

BV-BRC — Specialty genes (virulence, AMR, drug targets). Note: "Antibiotic Resistance: PATRIC" is BV-BRC's own internal curation, more permissive than CARD.

BacSPaD / UHGG — Body-site and genome data. BacSPaD reflects clinical-sequencing bias toward pathogens, not general microbiome ecology.

Known limitations

All associations are correlational unless marked ⚗ or ⚡. Correlation does not imply causation.

14 disease names appear under two different ontology IDs — confirmed genuine upstream ambiguities in the MONDO/MedDRA ontologies, not pipeline errors.

Geography coverage is ~9% — PubMed MeSH country tagging is sparse for microbiome literature.

Body-site sampling location affects interpretability: an organism "increased" in a disease found via blood sampling may reflect systemic effects, not direct gut involvement.

Ontology badges

MONDO — Disease mapped to a real MONDO ontology ID. Enables cross-database comparisons.

unmapped — Disease stored under a MedDRA or EFO placeholder. Correct evidence, just not yet cross-referenced to MONDO.
